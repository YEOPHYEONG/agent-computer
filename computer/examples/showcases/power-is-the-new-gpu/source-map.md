# Source Map

## Scope

User-approved scope: broad AI public-market ecosystem, U.S.-led with global context, 2026-2030 horizon. Educational only. If the topic becomes too broad, prioritize electricity, grid, data center, cooling, and power-infrastructure bottlenecks around AI demand.

## Source Tiers

- Tier 1: official or primary sources, including government agencies, reliability authorities, grid operators, utility filings, company filings, and company technical/sustainability disclosures.
- Tier 2: reputable research, market intelligence, financial/energy journalism, industry bodies, and academic/preprint work with traceable methods.
- Tier 3: social posts, forums, blogs, unsourced aggregators, and weak signals. These are excluded from the evidence base except as leads.

## Excluded Or Weak Sources

- Reddit and social-media posts: excluded from evidence base.
- Wikipedia: excluded except for orientation.
- Unsourced "AI data centers caused X% price spike" articles: use only if traced to Monitoring Analytics, FERC, PJM, or other primary source.

## Source Gaps

- Public, facility-level AI electricity consumption is limited.
- Large-load pipelines often mix announced, requested, studied, contracted, and energized MW.
- AI-specific load is hard to separate from traditional cloud, crypto, enterprise computing, and broader industrial electrification.
- Cooling and water data are fragmented and often aggregated at company level.
- Market impact on stocks cannot be inferred from physical bottlenecks alone; valuation, margins, regulation, financing, and execution matter.
