## Text Coverage Guide

Text coverage means the approximate share of slide area occupied by meaningful text, labels, callouts, annotations, and content blocks.

Use as default ranges, not rigid quotas:

- cover / section / closing: 5-20%
- cinematic concept: 10-25%
- standard explanation: 20-40%
- comparison / process / diagnostic: 30-50%
- dense explainer / source map: 40-60%

Adjust per slide based on audience, readability, language, and source depth.

## Notes

- Standard content slides should not be only a headline plus one subtitle.
- The slide structure should change when the content demands it.
- Use visible text actively: cards, callouts, annotations, comparison rows, labeled loops, mini tables, quote panels, or diagnostic overlays.
- Cover, section divider, and closing slides may be sparse only when the role explicitly requires it.
- Do not add claims that are absent from the approved source package.
- Put only background details in notes, README, or source report. The main slide argument should be visible inside the image.
